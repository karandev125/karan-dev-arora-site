Ousterhout's central idea is that complexity is the thing software design should fight most directly. The useful part is how practical that framing becomes: narrow interfaces, deep modules, explicit names, and a bias toward designs that make the next change easier.

The book is short, but it has the density of a long argument. I like it most as a vocabulary builder. It gives names to instincts that are otherwise easy to leave vague.

## Highlights

_Complexity is more apparent to readers than to writers._

_A good module is deep: a simple interface hiding meaningful behavior._
